Memo Writing

When to use memo

  • To issue instructions
  • To inform the employees about policy changes.
  • To give suggestions
  • To request support or information to confirm a decision made on telephone etc.

Types

  • Request memo
  • Confirmation memo - written to confirm a certain agreemen
  • Suggestive memo - written in requesting views from employees about a certain issue
  • Report memo - written to give an account of the progress using values and charts.
  • Informal results memo - written to give the results of a certain action.

Parts of memo

Heading

  • To
  • From
  • Date
  • Subject

Opening

the purpose, the context, and the specific assignment or task, mentioned in brief; approximately in the length of a short paragraph.

Body

The paragraph/s providing the relevant information clearly under headings.

Conclusion

courteously stating the action you want the reader to take
