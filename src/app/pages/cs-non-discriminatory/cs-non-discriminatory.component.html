Non Discriminatory

usage

  • when writing a female name

    Use ‘Ms’ in preference to ‘Miss/Mrs’ unless this is preferred by the individual

  • use plural form

    • a writer -> writers

    • his -> their

  • Rewriting the affected noun

    A student must present his ... --> X

    A student must present student's ... --> ✔

  • Replacing the offending pronoun with the definite article 'the'

    The lecturer will display his timetable on his door --> X

    The lecturer will display the timetable on the door. --> ✔

  • Eliminating the offending pronouns (Only if the original noun is plural)

    An executive should read his e-mails sent from clients daily. --> X

    An executive should read e-mails sent from clients daily.

  • Replacing the offending pronoun with the generic pronoun, 'one'.

    A staff member in Argentina earns less than he would in New York. --> X

    A staff member in Argentina earns less than one would in New York.

  • Rewriting using the relative pronoun 'who'

    A grocery store offers discounts to a customer when he buys more than ... --> X

    A grocery store offers discounts to a customer who buys more than ...

  • Rewording the sections where the offending pronouns are used

    The assumption of many e-mail users is that if a writer corrects his grammar and proofreads his document, he is wasting time. --> X

    The assumption of many e-mail users is that correcting grammar and proofreading a document, is a wastage of time. --> ✔

stereotypical nouns

Occupational titles

  • Headmistress /headmaster -> principal
  • Chairman -> chairperson / chair
  • Policeman -> police officer
  • Businessman -> business person
  • Fireman -> firefighter
  • Steward /stewardess -> flight attendant
  • Craftsman -> artisan
  • Salesman /saleswoman -> sales clerk, salesperson

Inherently sexist word

  • Mankind -> humanity, humankind, people, humans
  • Man-made -> synthetic, artificial
  • The common man -> the common / average person, ordinary people

Physical and or intellectual inability

  • Blind musician -> a musician with a vision impairment
  • A disabled person -> a person with special needs / differently abled person
  • Deaf -> with hearing impairments
