Tenses

Simple Present Tense

  • Repeated actions

    Do you visit your uncle every Sunday?

  • Habitual actions

    He often speaks in French

  • Facts that are believed to be true

    New York is a small city

  • General truths

    Sun sets from Westh

  • Scheduled events in near future

    The plane takes off at 10 o'clock tonight

Present Progressive/Continuous Tense

am/is/are + -ing

  • Actions that are happening now

    You are learning English now

  • Actions which are in progress but not right now

    I am reading the book Tom Sawyer these days

  • Actions that will or will not happen in near future.

    Are you meeting some friends after work?

Present Perfect Tense

has/have + past participle

  • Experiences

    I have watched that movie.

    I have been to France

  • Changes over time

    1. My English has really improved

    2. The government has become more interested in physical education

  • Accomplishments

    I have bought a gift for my friend

  • Incomplete actions at present (*Negative form)

    The rain hasn’t stopped.

  • Multiple actions at different times in past

    I have had four quizzes and five tests so far this semester

  • Actions that have just ended or already done

    He has just/already completed his assignment

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

has/have been + -ing

  • Actions which started in the past and continue till present. Time duration is a must.

    1. They have been talking for the last two hours

    2. He has been teaching at the university since June

  • Actions which started in the past and have been happening lately.

    She has been working a lot recently.

Simple Past Tense

  • Completed actions in the past

    The dog chased the rabbit

  • A series of completed actions

    I went to work, had breakfast, and started working.

  • Habits in the past

    Chamal went to the library every Sunday when he was young

Negative

  • he did not do that

Interrogative

  • Did he do that ?

Past Continuous/ Progressive tense

was/were + -ing

  • Expresses actions in progress in the past

    He was reading a book when his dog jumped into his lap

  • Temporary actions in the past

    I was working in the bank for two weeks

Past Perfect Tense

had + past participle

  • Expresses a past action that happened before some other past action

    The meeting had already started by the time I arrived

Past Perfect Continuous/Progressive Tense

had been + -ing

  • an action that started in the past, continued in the past, and also ended at a certain point in the past

    She had been driving around the city before she finally found the correct office.

Simple Future Tense

will + verb

  • What we expect to happen in the future.

    It will take 50 years for the temperature to return to normal

  • Spontaneous decisions

    Come on, I will help you to sort out the files

  • A promise, warning or threat

    I will do it tomorrow

going to ...

am/is/are + going to + verb

  • The sky is getting darker. I think it is going to rain.
  • I am going to drive to work today

Future Progressive Tense

will + be + -ing

  • An action which will be in progress, which is certain to happen

    Next Monday you will be working in your new job

  • When politely asking for information related to future

    Will you be bringing your friend to the class tomorrow?

Future Perfect Tense

will have + past participle

  • A completed action in future. Often used with the time expression, by or by the time

    By the time we get home, the match will have begun

  • project ourselves forward in time and to look back.

    Oh no, we want to go on a bike ride in an hour. Don’t worry, he will have repaired the bike by then.

Future Perfect Progressive Tense

will have been + -ing

  • An action that will continue up until a point in the future.

    When I turn thirty, I will have been playing piano for twenty-one years

  • The activity is currently unfinished and is expected to continue in the future.

    I will have been waiting here for three hours by six o'clock
